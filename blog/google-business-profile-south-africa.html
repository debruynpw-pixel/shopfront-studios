How to Set Up Google Business Profile in South Africa (And Actually Rank)

By PW de Bruyn 21 July 2026 7 min read

If you run a small business in South Africa and you only ever do one marketing thing, do this one. A properly built Google Business Profile is free, takes an afternoon to set up, and is the single highest-leverage thing available to a local business. It puts you in the map pack — those three listings with the little map that sit above every organic result when someone searches “plumber near me” or “accountant Centurion”. Most SA businesses either never claim theirs or claim it and then abandon it half-finished. That gap is your opening.

Short answer

Google Business Profile is a free listing that places a business in Google Maps and the local map pack. In South Africa, set it up at google.com/business, verify by video (one to five working days), choose the most specific primary category, and build reviews steadily. Category, proximity and reviews decide map pack rankings.

What is Google Business Profile and why does it matter more than your website?

Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) is the free listing that controls how your business appears in Google Maps and in the block of three local results at the top of a search. It holds your name, address, phone number, hours, photos, reviews, services and a link to your site.

It matters more than your website in one specific and important way: it gets seen first. When someone in Roodepoort searches “geyser repair”, the map pack sits above every organic result on desktop and takes up most of the first screen on a phone. Roughly nine in ten of those searches happen on a phone, and a large share of them end in a tap on the call button without anyone ever opening a website.

So the profile is where the demand lands. But it is not where the deal closes. That is the part people get wrong — more on that further down.

The other reason to take it seriously: it costs nothing. No listing fee, no verification fee, no paid tier. Anyone phoning you to demand payment to “keep your listing active” or to “remove a competitor” is running a scam that is very common in South Africa. Google does not phone SMEs for money.

How do you set it up and get verified in South Africa?

Go to google.com/business, sign in with the Google account you actually want to own this long-term (not a staff member's personal Gmail), and search for your business name first. Half the time a listing already exists — Google auto-generates them from directories and user submissions. If yours is there, claim it rather than creating a duplicate.

Video verification trips people up, so do it properly. One continuous recording, no cuts, showing three things in sequence: the outside of your premises or your branded vehicle with a street sign or recognisable landmark visible; proof you are genuinely operating (tools in the bakkie, stock on shelves, equipment in use); and proof you are the owner — keys, opening a till, accessing a back office, or holding business documentation. Do not narrate a sales pitch. Just show it.

Category: the setting that matters most

Your primary category has more influence on your map pack ranking than anything else you control. Choose the most specific one that fits. “Plumber” beats “Contractor”. “Roof plumber” or “Drainage service” beats “Plumber” if that is genuinely your core work. Then add secondary categories for the rest of what you do — they help, but nowhere near as much.

Test it: search your main keyword in Maps, click the top three competitors and look at the category listed under their name. If everyone ranking says “Electrician” and you have chosen “Electrical installation service”, you have picked the wrong one.

Address, service areas, hours and photos

  • Service-area business: if you travel to clients, register as a service-area business. Enter your address so Google can verify you, then hide it from public view and list the suburbs and towns you serve instead. List 10–20 real areas, not the whole province.
  • Storefront: if customers come to you, show the full address and make sure the map pin is dropped on the actual door, not the middle of the block.
  • Hours: fill them in accurately, including public holidays. “Open 24 hours” when you are not gets you one-star reviews from people who phoned at 11pm.
  • Photos: upload at least 15 to start — exterior, team, vehicle, work in progress, and clear before-and-after shots. Real photos taken on a phone beat stock images every time. Stock photography is obvious and Google users scroll straight past it.

What actually decides who ranks in the map pack?

Google weighs three things: relevance (does your category and content match the search), distance (how close you are to the searcher), and prominence (reviews, links, mentions, activity). You cannot move your business closer to every searcher, but you can influence almost everything else.

A few of these need explaining.

Reviews are not just a total. Google looks at how many you have, how fast they arrive, how recent the latest ones are, and what words they contain. Forty reviews with the newest one from 2023 is a weaker signal than twelve reviews spread evenly across the past six months. And a review that says “fixed a burst geyser in Sandton on a Sunday” carries keyword and location signal that “great service, thanks” does not.

NAP consistency means your Name, Address and Phone number appear identically everywhere online — your website, Facebook, Yellow Pages, Snupit, industry directories. Google cross-references these. Mismatches make it less confident the business is real, and confidence is what it is scoring. Pick one phone format and stick to it everywhere: either +27 79 022 7258 or 079 022 7258, never a mix of both.

Google Posts and Q&A barely move rankings, but they do move conversions. A post announcing after-hours callouts, or a Q&A entry you seeded yourself answering “Do you charge a callout fee?”, changes whether the person who found you actually phones. Seed five or six honest questions and answer them from your business account. It is allowed and it works.

How the profile and the website feed each other

The profile wins the map pack. The website wins the organic results underneath it and closes the trust gap for people who click through before phoning. They compound.

Your profile links to your site, which tells Google what your business does in far more detail than a category dropdown allows. Suburb-specific service pages on your site strengthen your relevance for searches in those suburbs. Then in the other direction, clicks and calls from the profile are engagement signals, and a site that loads fast and answers questions keeps those visitors instead of bouncing them back to a competitor. A profile pointing at a slow or thin site underperforms a profile pointing at a solid one. If you are weighing up what that side costs, we broke it down in how much a website costs in South Africa.

Four mistakes SA businesses make constantly

  1. Home address showing publicly on a mobile business. If you work from a bakkie, hide the address. Beyond the obvious safety issue, Google may suspend a listing that shows a residential address as a storefront.
  2. Wrong primary category. A tiling specialist listed under “General contractor” will lose to every tiler in the suburb, permanently, no matter how many reviews they collect.
  3. Inconsistent phone formats. +27 on the profile, 0-prefix on the website, an old landline on Facebook. Fix all three to match.
  4. Duplicate listings. Usually created when someone claims a profile and later forgets, then makes a fresh one. Duplicates split your reviews and can get both suspended. Search your business name and address in Maps, then report any duplicate through “Suggest an edit → Close or remove → Duplicate”.

How do you get reviews without pestering customers?

Reviews are the only ranking factor that requires other people, which is why most businesses avoid asking. Three rules make it painless.

Ask at the moment of delivered value. Not in a monthly newsletter, not three weeks later. The instant the job is done and the customer is visibly happy — drain cleared, invoice settled, keys handed over. That is the only window where a stranger cheerfully does you a favour. A trade doing five jobs a day who asks at every completion will out-review a competitor with ten times the marketing budget inside three months.

Remove all friction. Get your review short link from your profile dashboard (Ask for reviews → it generates a g.page/r/... URL) and send it on WhatsApp with one line: “Thanks Johan — if you have 30 seconds, a quick Google review helps us a lot: [link]”. Do not ask people to search for you. Do not send a PDF. One tap should open the star rating.

Reply to every review, good and bad. Replies are visible, they signal an active business, and they let you naturally use your service and suburb words. On a bad review, respond calmly with facts and an offer to sort it out. Prospects read the reply more closely than the complaint — a measured response to an angry review sells better than a wall of perfect fives.

What not to do: never buy reviews and never post fakes from family accounts. Google detects clustered review patterns from the same devices, IP ranges and dormant accounts, and it suppresses them — often silently. You pay for reviews that get filtered out, and you put the whole listing at risk of suspension. There is no version of this that works.

A realistic target for a small SA service business: two to four genuine reviews a month, forever. That beats a burst of twenty followed by silence, because velocity and recency both count.

Set a monthly rhythm and it stops being a project: upload three photos, publish one post, answer any new questions, reply to every review. Twenty minutes. That is the whole maintenance job.

Frequently asked questions

Is Google Business Profile free in South Africa?

Yes. Creating, verifying and managing a Google Business Profile costs nothing. There is no paid tier, no listing fee and no verification fee. If someone phones you demanding payment to keep your listing active or to remove a competitor, it is a scam. Google will never phone you for money to maintain a profile.

How long does Google Business Profile verification take in South Africa?

Video verification is the most common method in South Africa and is usually reviewed within one to five working days. Phone or SMS verification, when offered, is instant. Postcard verification takes two to four weeks via the South African Post Office and often fails to arrive, so choose video if Google gives you the option.

Can I run a Google Business Profile without showing my home address?

Yes. Register as a service-area business, enter your address during setup so Google can verify you, then tick the option to hide it from the public. Your profile will show the suburbs and towns you serve instead of a street address. Plumbers, electricians and mobile trades should always do this.

Do I still need a website if I have a Google Business Profile?

Yes. Google Business Profile wins you the map pack, but the website closes the trust gap. Profiles that link to a real site with service pages, pricing and proof rank better in the map pack and convert far more of the clicks they get. The two feed each other rather than replacing one another.
